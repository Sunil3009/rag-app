SECTION 16: TANK STATUS RULES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

SAFE: Fill level 50% or above
WARNING: Fill level between 30% and 49%
CRITICAL: Fill level below 30%

When fill level equals or falls below the customer's threshold percentage, priority is escalated to HIGH or CRITICAL and dispatch is triggered immediately.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 17: CONTRACT TYPES EXPLAINED
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Take-or-Pay:
  - Customer must pay for a minimum volume of gas regardless of usage.
  - If supplier fails to deliver, a penalty per hour is charged.
  - Highest financial risk contract for the supplier if delivery is missed.
  - Example: Helios Steel Works — £14,200/hr penalty.

Long-Term:
  - Fixed-duration supply agreement with agreed SLA response times.
  - Penalties apply for late delivery but terms are less punitive than Take-or-Pay.
  - Example: BioPharm Solutions — £8,600/hr, CryoMed Hospital — £22,000/hr.

Spot:
  - Ad-hoc delivery with no long-term commitment.
  - Flexible pricing; no recurring penalty clauses.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 18: PRIORITY LEVELS EXPLAINED
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

CRITICAL:
  - Tank level is at or below threshold.
  - Hours to empty is typically under 10 hours.
  - Immediate dispatch required.
  - Penalty clock is active or imminent.

HIGH:
  - Tank level approaching threshold.
  - Hours to empty typically between 5 and 15 hours.
  - Dispatch required within current run.

MEDIUM:
  - Tank level above threshold with sufficient buffer.
  - Hours to empty above 15 hours.
  - Can be served later in the delivery sequence.
